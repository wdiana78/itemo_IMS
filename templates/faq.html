This page answers common questions about ITEMO IMS as a central inventory management system: what it does, who it is for, and how teams use it to keep stock organised across projects, branches, and warehouses. Use it as a quick guide before you reach out through the contact page.

Core questions

ITEMO IMS is a web-based inventory management system used to record, track, and control stock in one central place. It is designed for stock-heavy teams across industries such as construction, retail, healthcare, and distribution, giving them a single source of truth for quantities, locations, and movements.

ITEMO IMS is designed to act as your central inventory management service for day-to-day work. It supports receiving, issuing, and monitoring stock across locations and can scale from a single store to multiple projects or branches as your organisation grows.

ITEMO IMS can be configured to support data exports and secure backups so that your inventory records remain safe and easy to analyse. Depending on your setup, you can generate CSV or Excel files for items, orders, issues, and payments, and connect your account to regular backup routines.

Quick tips
  • Use clear, consistent names for items, locations, and suppliers.
  • Record deliveries and issues on the same day to keep stock accurate.
  • Review the dashboard for low stock before raising purchase orders.

Getting started
  • Create a few sample items and categories that match your industry.
  • Add at least one supplier and one client, site, or department.
  • Record your first order, delivery, and issue to experience the full flow.
Daily use checklist
  • Update deliveries that arrived during the day in ITEMO IMS.
  • Log issues to projects, branches, or departments before closing store.
  • Glance at low stock and out-of-stock indicators and plan reorders.
When something changes
  • Update item descriptions or units when specifications change.
  • Mark inactive suppliers so they do not appear as primary options.
  • Review payment-related records when invoices are approved or settled.
