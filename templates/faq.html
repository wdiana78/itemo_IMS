Frequently asked questions

This page collects common questions about how ITEMO IMS works, who should use it, and what to expect from this demo version. Use it as a quick guide before you reach out through the contact page.

When to check this page
  • When you are not sure what the system is meant to do.
  • When you want to confirm how access and permissions work.
  • When you are testing the demo and want to know its limits.

Core questions

ITEMO IMS is used to record, track, and manage items and stock levels in one central system. Instead of scattered spreadsheets or paper notes, store and project teams share a single source of truth for quantities, status, and movements.

Only registered and logged in users can access the inventory dashboard. In a full production rollout, different roles such as storekeepers, project managers, and finance staff could be given different levels of access.

Yes. Users can create new items and records, view existing data, update details such as quantities or descriptions, and delete records that are no longer needed. This applies across core areas such as items, suppliers, clients, and orders.

This installation is a demo built for a web development project. The flows and screens are designed to feel realistic, but real deployments would include extra layers such as role-based permissions, backups, and integrations with external services.

In this demo, exports are limited. In a production version, it would be straightforward to add CSV or Excel exports for items, orders, and issues, so that teams can run reports or share data with finance and management.

Start by checking whether the item, supplier, or client was entered twice or with slightly different names. If the issue persists, use the Contact page to describe what you were doing, what you expected to see, and what actually happened. That information is very helpful when debugging.
Quick tips
  • Use clear, consistent names for items and sites.
  • Record deliveries and issues on the same day where possible.
  • Review the dashboard for low stock before placing orders.

Getting started
  • Create a few sample items and categories.
  • Add at least one supplier and one client or department.
  • Record a test order, delivery, and issue to see the full flow.
Daily use checklist
  • Update deliveries that arrived during the day.
  • Log issues to sites before closing the store.
  • Glance at low stock and out-of-stock indicators.
When something changes
  • Update item descriptions if specifications change.
  • Mark inactive suppliers so they do not appear by default.
  • Review payment records when invoices are settled.
